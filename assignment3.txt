SE4471 Course Project – Assignment 3
Final System, Reliability Report, and Showcase Demo
Total Weight: 20%
Date: April 9 (during scheduled lab)
Format: In-person team presentation + final submission
Purpose
Phase 3 represents the completion of your EventOps AI application.
Teams will submit their final system, a reliability and evaluation report, and present a live demonstration.
This phase evaluates:
the quality and completeness of your system,
your ability to communicate design and workflow, and
your understanding of reliability and system limitations
Final Deliverable Components
Deliverable 3 consists of three graded components:
1. Product (Application) — 10%
Fully implemented EventOps AI system
Includes:
RAG pipeline
multi-step workflow
context/state tracking
structured output artifact
2. Demo (Presentation) — 5%
5–7 minute in-lab presentation
Demonstration of workflow, system behavior, and outputs
3. Report (Reliability and Evaluation) — 5%
Testing strategy
Failure cases and limitations
Prompt/workflow design decisions
Evidence of retrieval grounding
Note: The report is graded independently and is not bounded by implementation tier.
Implementation Tiers (Grade Boundaries)
The Product and Demo components (15%) are evaluated within the tier your system achieves.
Tier 1 – Foundation System (≈ mid-70% range)
Working RAG over event corpus
Basic multi-step workflow
Initial context/state tracking
Partial or developing structured artifact
Tier 2 – Tool-Enabled System (≈ mid-80% range)
Includes Tier 1 plus:
Integration of at least one external tool or API
Tool usage meaningfully embedded in workflow
Stronger end-to-end execution
Tier 3 – Advanced System (90%+ range)
Includes Tier 2 plus one or more:
Multi-tool orchestration
Advanced workflow design
MCP-based architecture or extensions
Optimization (latency, cost, retrieval scaling)
Additional engineering depth beyond baseline
Important Note on Tier-Based Grading
Product and Demo grades are bounded by the implementation tier achieved
Report grade is independent of tier
Showcase Demo (April 9)
Each team will deliver a 5–7 minute presentation.
Important
The presentation will be used to assess all components of Deliverable 3.
Teams are expected to clearly demonstrate:
the functionality of the product
the design and workflow of the system
the quality and relevance of outputs
the creativity and real-world value of the application
Presentation Structure
1. Application Motivation
Problem being solved
Why it is relevant and valuable
2. System Overview
RAG pipeline
workflow/agent logic
memory/state tracking
structured outputs
3. Workflow and AI Interaction
End-to-end interaction example
Decision-making and system flow
4. Demonstration
Live demo or short recording (if needed)
At least one complete workflow
Demo Guidelines
Keep demonstrations focused and efficient
Avoid long setup or delays
Ensure a clear, complete example is shown
Recordings should support—not replace—your explanation
Slides are encouraged to support y our presentation.
